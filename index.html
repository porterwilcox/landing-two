about

Experience and Work

I'm a full time staff member and instructor at CodeWorks in Boise, Idaho. As a former student and graduate of CodeWorks - class of summer 2018 - I am aware of how challenging the class is for students, because we teach to industry standards. This means that as an instructor I know both what the industry standards are and, most importantly, why we write our code accordingly. My emphasis on readability, scalability, and protection enables my works to function optimally throughout their duration.

At CodeWorks we teach both front end and back end web development. I am well versed in the languages and technologies necessary to produce modern full stack applications.

Beyond work, I enjoy creating a wide range of side portfolio from fully functional websites to virtual reality video games.

Personal

In 2016 I graduated from Boise State University with a bachelor's degree in philosophy. While earning my degree I most enjoyed the rigorous analytical thinking that solving logical proofs demanded. I couldn't have known then that Plato's theory of Forms would solidify my understanding of constructor functions or ES6 classes, or that the concepts frequenting symbolic logic would reemerge in programming encapsulation. Studying philosophy taught me how to develop and cultivate new frameworks of thought, which I've utilized immensely within the software industry.

My passions other than software development are rock climbing, mountain biking/touring, soccer, and camping.

I'm commonly found in good company amongst friends and family around backyard campfires.
